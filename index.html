This is the demo of an yet to be funded open source iniative. Read more.

The goal is to have an editor / platform with the ability to communicate in models on the foundation of existing, scientific, yet abstract facts. In comparison to online communication in pure text, a model allows the reader to adapt it interactively and reactively by just dragging the mouse over the numbers.

This version here is basically just a draft of what the displayed model can look like and for what it could be used. It is based on Tangle, which works ok for this use case. But what is needed most, is an super user friendly editor to create and edit models on the foundation of scientific facts and conversion of units.

The underlying belief is, that this can shape public discourse in a complex world (with challenges like corona and climate change), because it invites everyone to understand and work on the models (rather than opinions) and make their own assumptions (what moves the lever and what doesn't).

Reactive Documents Assumption Tool Demo

Assumption #5: Data avoidance over intense tracking and bloated library usage saves a significant amount of CO₂.

Assumption

  • By being efficient with libraries and good code, you can reduce the size of a deployment on the web by kb.
  • If you have .000.000 views per month, and 1 GB data transfer costs Wh electricity, you save kg CO₂

Perspective

Is that a lot? Is it little? Since a user can't do anything with the plain number, it makes sense to provide some comparisons regarding the impact.

  • kg CO₂ = Driving km with a car (with g CO₂ per km of driving)

Facts

The system needs to provide some common denominators, because they are scientifically proven. So the user does not have to research these basic facts again and again.

  • 1GB data transfer consumes 0,5kWh = 500Wh of energy.
  • 1kWh of energy produced by the current European electricity grid equals 0,295kg CO₂
  • In 2010, the average value of emitted CO₂ of newly registered passenger cars was 151.7 g/km

Discussion

The discussion takes place publicly on Twitter by having a corresponding unique hashtag for each assumption.
